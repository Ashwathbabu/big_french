TEF vs TCF: Which Exam Should You Take?

A detailed comparison of both Canadian French exams to help you choose the right one for your immigration pathway — with a clear recommendation at the end.

BF
BigFrench Expert Team
TEF & TCF Canada Preparation Specialists

Both TEF Canada and TCF Canada are accepted by IRCC for Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and Canadian citizenship applications. But they're quite different exams in format, scoring, and difficulty — and choosing the wrong one could cost you weeks of preparation. Here's everything you need to know.

TEF Canada

Test d'Évaluation de Français

By CCI Paris Île-de-France

TCF Canada

Test de Connaissance du Français

By France Éducation International

Full Side-by-Side Comparison

Feature TEF Canada TCF Canada
Administered by CCI Paris Île-de-France France Éducation International

Scoring System TEF levels → converted to NCLC NCLC directly
Listening 60 questions / 40 min 29 questions / 25 min
Reading 50 questions / 60 min 39 questions / 60 min
Writing Tasks Article + formal letter Email + argumentative essay

Choose TEF Canada If...

  • You're comfortable with longer, more structured speaking tasks
  • You've studied formal French writing — letters, articles, formal correspondence
  • You prefer more questions with clear right/wrong answers in Listening & Reading
  • You have 3+ months to prepare from a beginner/intermediate level
  • You prefer methodical, step-by-step preparation with a predictable format

Choose TCF Canada If...

  • You prefer shorter, faster-paced tests with fewer questions per module
  • You're comfortable writing emails and argumentative essays in French
  • You want NCLC scoring directly — no confusing conversion tables
  • You're preparing under time pressure (1–2 months available)
  • You're already conversational in French and want an efficient exam

The 3 Key Differences That Actually Matter

1

Speaking Style is Very Different

TEF speaking requires you to sustain a 4–5 minute monologue and engage in a structured role-play — this rewards students who can speak at length. TCF speaking uses 3 shorter tasks that test direct communication. If you get nervous speaking for long stretches, TCF may suit you better.

2

Scoring Transparency

TCF gives you NCLC levels directly — you know exactly where you stand immediately. TEF uses its own scoring system that must be converted to NCLC, which adds a layer of complexity and can feel opaque. If you want clarity, TCF wins here.

3

Volume of Listening & Reading Questions

TEF has significantly more questions — 60 listening vs TCF's 29, and 50 reading vs TCF's 39. More questions mean more opportunities to recover from errors, but also more fatigue. For students who struggle with stamina, TCF's shorter format is more manageable.
