FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before getting started.

TEF (Test d'Évaluation de Français) is primarily used for Canadian immigration and citizenship. TCF (Test de Connaissance du Français) is used for academic admissions and French citizenship. Both assess the same language skills but have different formats.
Our mock tests are designed by certified French language experts who have extensive experience with both TEF and TCF exams. They closely mirror the real exam format, timing, difficulty level, and question types — so when you walk into the official exam, it feels familiar. Over 98% of our students report that our practice tests were highly representative of the real exam.
Preparation time depends on your current French level and your target score. Generally, we recommend at least 4–8 weeks of consistent daily practice. Students starting from A2–B1 level typically need 2–3 months to reach CLB 9+. Our diagnostic test will assess your level and give you a personalized study timeline from day one.
