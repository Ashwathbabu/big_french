TEF vs TCF: Which Exam Should You Take?

A detailed comparison of both exams to help you choose the right one for your immigration pathway.

BF
BigFrench Experts
TEF & TCF Preparation Specialists

Both TEF Canada and TCF Canada are accepted by IRCC for Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and citizenship applications. But they're quite different in format, scoring, and difficulty — so choosing the right one matters.

Key Differences at a Glance

Feature TEF Canada TCF Canada
Speaking formatRole-play + monologue3 structured tasks
Writing tasksArticle + formal letterEmail + essay
Listening60 Qs / 40 min29 Qs / 25 min
Reading50 Qs / 60 min39 Qs / 60 min
Score validity2 years2 years

Choose TEF Canada If...

  • You're comfortable with longer, more structured speaking tasks
  • You've studied formal French writing (letters, articles)
  • You prefer more questions with clear right/wrong answers in Listening & Reading
  • You have 3+ months to prepare

Choose TCF Canada If...

  • You prefer shorter, faster-paced tests
  • You're comfortable writing emails and argumentative essays
  • You want NCLC scoring directly (no conversion needed)
  • You're preparing under time pressure

Our Recommendation

If you're a beginner or have a structured study approach, TEF Canada is generally more predictable and easier to prepare for. If you're already conversational in French and want a shorter exam, TCF Canada may suit you better.
